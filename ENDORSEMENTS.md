## Peter Cranstone — 3PMobile

**Role:** CEO, 3PMobile. Building web-standards infrastructure for consumer-controlled engagement, trusted execution, and ecosystem-scale growth.

**Contribution:** Peter identified a critical unresolved gap in the v0.1 specification in April 2026, articulating a distinction that has materially sharpened Open Problem 1.

His formulation, reproduced with permission:

> "What is the durable artifact of lawful commitment itself? A decision graph, validator mesh, and immutable audit trail can explain and defend an outcome. But if the next domain still has to reconstruct why the act was valid, then the act itself does not hold."

And the refinement that followed:

> "A cryptographically signed artifact carrying authorisation, purpose compatibility, and confidence is a major step forward — but it may still be an evidence-bearing object unless lawful commitment itself becomes the primary object of reliance. Not just a portable explanation of why the act should be trusted, but what must exist such that the act can be encountered as already standing."

This distinction — between a **portable explanation** and a **portable act** — defines the boundary of what the current five-layer architecture can and cannot achieve. In practical terms, it introduces a second failure mode: a decision can be valid at the point of binding and still fail once it crosses into another domain, because it degrades into something that must be re-evaluated rather than relied upon.

Peter is actively working on related problems at 3PMobile. We welcome his continued engagement with this specification.
